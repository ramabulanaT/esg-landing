The ESG Reckoning: the silent tax on laggards

The era of checkbox ESG is over. IFRS S1/S2, EU CSRD, TNFD and South African sector rules have rewired both regulation and capital. Laggards are already paying a silent tax in higher capital costs, insurance premiums, stranded assets and talent churn.

Capital penalty

ESG laggards can face a higher cost of capital. For example, on a hypothetical R100M facility, a 1.2% premium would mean over R6M in additional interest over five years.

Cost of capital is now directly tied to ESG performance.

Operational fragility

Without supplier ESG auditing, organisations face 30–40% more disruptions by 2026, with higher insurance premiums and greater exposure to environmental and social incidents.

Supply chains and communities are now ESG-sensitive.

Talent & reputation

ESG laggards experience 20–30% higher turnover of skilled professionals and growing reputational drag with investors, customers and regulators.

Employees and stakeholders are voting with their feet.

Is your organisation quietly an ESG laggard?

If three or more of these statements are true, your organisation sits in the laggard category – and every month of delay adds 15–20% to your eventual cost of catching up.

Laggard indicators

  • ESG is treated as a communications / CSR function, not strategic.
  • There is no dedicated Board-level sustainability / ESG committee.
  • Carbon emissions are tracked inconsistently, or not at all.
  • ESG reporting is annual and retrospective, not continuous.
  • Third-party assurance is minimal or absent.
  • Data sits in spreadsheets and email, not in a system of record.
  • No science-based targets or credible transition plan.
  • Stakeholder engagement is reactive, not proactive.

Three or more apply? You are a laggard – by definition.
